ICD-10 Migration, Adoption and Compliance - Syntel's nSure10SM

The ICD-10 mandate presents healthcare payers and providers with one of the largest, most complex regulatory challenges to date. ICD-10 migration is a mission-critical project and must be well-planned.

This monumental change presents many challenges. Understanding these is critical to a successful migration. Some of the major challenges include:

  • 5010 Migration
  • Impact Analysis
  • Modifications to business rules
  • ICD-9 to 10 mapping
  • Dual coding structures during transition
  • Internal and external testing

An extensive amount of payer and provider business processes and systems are impacted by the ICD-10 migration. Both technology applications and business processes will have to be modified to accept and utilize ICD-10 codes. Syntel’s end-to-end offering – nSure10 – is an integrated business and technology solution that covers the entire payer and provider value chains.

  • Payers: pre-sales, underwriting, claims adjudication and care management
  • Providers: revenue cycle, clinical and financial management

Syntel is well-equipped to help you develop a roadmap and navigate your team to compliance - and beyond. Our phased approach to ICD-10 adoption will build on basic compliance help you elevate your investment to transform your organization. Addressing compliance, increased utilization and creating market differentiators, our adoption phases are Assessment and Blueprint, Remediation, and Testing.

ICD-10 Testing: Blend of a well-defined life cycle approach and processes that leverage accelerators and tools to expedite the complete testing effort, including a customizable test scenario repository.

Neutrality Risk Analysis: The migration from ICD-9 to ICD-10 will expose providers to financial risks in terms of the reimbursements that they will receive from the payers for conditions treated today and coded in ICD-9 vis a vis the same condition treated post Oct 1, 2014 and coded in ICD-10. For a successful migration, it is imperative for organizations to evaluate the potential financial impact of moving to ICD-10 much beyond Oct 2014. Syntel’s financial risk modeling will aid the providers in comprehending the exact nature of the reimbursement variation.

Neutrality Testing: While migrating to ICD-10 it is crucial to avoid payout variations and remain ICD-10 Neutral from a financial perspective, as well as in key operations such as medical policy enforcement. Syntel’s neutrality risk analysis and modeling framework helps healthcare organizations achieve neutrality by understanding and mitigating risks at each stage of their migration.
